My Library Story

I am My Library and I have 2 main objectives in our app: I am the single hub screen where users can find and browse all the content related to their app usage, for all content types, and I am also the place where users will find their account information, with their current user status (guest, subscribed, provider), and the other app info usual suspects: FAQ, Contact Us and About.

The app info items are very important even though seldom used so I display them in their own swimlane at the top of my screen.

The first card of the App Info category, Account and Settings, brings users to one of my children, Account and Settings. FAQ and Contact Us, the second and third cards are links to web pages. Finally, the About card is also a link to another child of mine, About, where users can view the current version of our app along with 2 links to web pages for our Terms & Conditions and our Privacy Policy.

The content of this swimlane being so different from the other swimlanes displaying media assets, I have decided to prepend its name with a gear icon to help quickly identify its purpose (it used to be its own main navigation section, named App Info, with the same gear icon prior to being integrated as one of my swimlanes).

It was a bit of a hard pill to swallow for App Info, who felt he was being demoted but he totally understood our decision, seeing as there is so much personalized usage content to be displayed, with music, podcasts, radio, history, favorites, downloads, etc.

The rest of my swimlanes are exclusively dedicated to user content, starting with Music History.

On initial launch, even when it has yet to be populated, Music History is displayed just below App Info. At first, it only displays ghost cards with a history icon that will be replaced with cards of music channels our user has listened to, in chronological order (most recent to oldest).

Exceptionally, I also always display a More button in Music History’s header because users must be able to access the grid view of their music history since it is the screen where they can clear their music history at any time.

After this swimlane comes Your Music Channels where users can see any music channel they have liked. Note that this swimlane has the standard More button display where it only appears when the category has more cards than the maximum number of cards in a swimlane.

Podcasts have a few more podcasts and episodes related swimlanes. I start with Your Podcast History followed by You Podcasts, featuring shows that the user has subscribed to (similar to Music swimlanes: History then Favorites).

Then come the episode swimlanes: Continue Listening for in progress episodes, Your Episodes for bookmarked episodes, New Episodes for the latest episodes of subscribed podcasts and Downloaded Episodes.

And last but not least, I display 2 radio swimlanes: Your Radio History and Your Radio Stations.

That’s a lot of users driven swimlanes and that’s one of the reasons I was called in to help with grouping and displaying all these different user customized content swimlanes under a single roof.

It might look overwhelming at first but remember that these will grow with usage. At first, I only display the empty History swimlanes for Music, Podcasts and Radio.

The other categories are displayed on a need basis as they are being populated by users, so I am not daunting; users understand what is going on with me since the extent of the user customized swimlanes display only grows with their usage.

In the end, I am very excited to help users focus on their customized through usage content in one easy to find place, along with their settings and account info.
