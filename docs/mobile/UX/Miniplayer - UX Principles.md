# SM Miniplayer – UX Principles

## Purpose

The SM Miniplayer extends the full player experience beyond its dedicated screen, enabling continuous, lightweight interaction with streaming content while users browse the app.

---

## Core UX Principles

### 1. Always Available, Never Intrusive

- The miniplayer is persistently accessible once content is playing, ensuring continuous control without forcing a context switch.
- Its compact form and footer placement provide utility without stealing attention from the user’s primary task.

**UX intent:** Support multitasking and content continuity while respecting user focus.

---

### 2. Clear Relationship to the Full Player

- The miniplayer is explicitly positioned as a lightweight counterpart to the full player.
- Tapping controls keeps users in context; tapping the miniplayer surface transitions clearly and predictably to the full player.

**UX intent:** Create a strong mental model where the miniplayer feels like a gateway, not a separate experience.

---

### 3. Familiar and Predictable Placement

- Persistently anchored at the bottom of the screen, just above the main navigation.
- Consistent placement ensures quick discovery and muscle memory across all content types and screens.

**UX intent:** Reduce cognitive load through spatial consistency.

---

### 4. Adaptive to Content Type

- Dynamically adjusts controls based on what is playing:
  - **Music:** Play / Pause, Skip
  - **Podcasts:** 15s back, Play / Pause, 30s forward
  - **Radio:** Play / Pause

**UX intent:** Deliver only what is most useful in the moment, avoiding unnecessary complexity.

---

### 5. Task‑Focused Control Set

- Displays only the most frequently used actions alongside essential metadata (thumbnail and labels).
- Advanced or exploratory controls are intentionally deferred to the full player.

**UX intent:** Optimize speed and ease for common actions.

---

### 6. Context Preservation

- Interacting with player controls does not disrupt navigation or browsing.
- Users maintain their current location in the app while managing playback.

**UX intent:** Let users stay in flow while staying connected to their content.

---

### 7. Intentional Escalation to Immersion

- A deliberate tap on the miniplayer surface triggers a clear wipe‑up animation to the full player.
- The transition visually signals a shift from browsing to focused listening.

**UX intent:** Reinforce mode changes through motion and hierarchy.

---

### 8. Strong Visual Contrast and Legibility

- Uses an inverse color scheme relative to the app’s theme (light/dark).
- Ensures readability and clear separation from underlying content.

**UX intent:** Maintain accessibility and clarity in all visual contexts.

---

### 9. Consistent Identity Across Content

- Looks and behaves the same whether playing music, podcasts, or radio.
- Reinforces recognition and trust through consistency.

**UX intent:** Make the miniplayer instantly recognizable regardless of content type.

---

## In Summary

The SM Miniplayer is designed as an optimized, respectful extension of the full player: always present, highly usable, and deliberately restrained. It empowers users to stay engaged with their audio while continuing their journey through the app, and serves as a clear, accessible bridge to deeper playback experiences.
