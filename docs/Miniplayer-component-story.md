I am the miniplayer and I am proud to always be there for users to interact with the streaming content when they are away from my older brother, the full player screen.

Any time users start streaming any content, my brother will take the spotlight in all his full screen glory but then, I will always be there, ready and only waiting for users to dismiss my older brother before showing up.

I am not as full-fledged a player as my brother, of course, but I still offer basic player controls in a much more ubiquitous way without being obnoxious: I’m there to support users when they are searching or browsing in the app, for example, so they don’t have to interrupt what their journey to go visit the full player screen.

At any time, I can display what is currently playing no matter the content type because I easily adapt to music channels, podcast episodes or radio stations. Users will always recognize me and find me waiting at the bottom of the screen.

To manage that, I had to negotiate prime space positioning right at the top of my friend, the app footer who let me sit right on top of our mutual friend, another member of the footer gang: the main navigation bar.

My modus operandi is to let users stay where they are in the app when they tap my basic player controls to interact with the current stream but as soon as they tap me anywhere else, I will gladly yield to my older brother who has much more experience and know-how regarding player features.

In that case, he will proudly transition over me again, and the rest of the screen, to show himself in a wipe up transition from the bottom to the top of the screen, proudly hiding anything else on the screen, so users can focus on what is currently playing.

So what are my player controls, exactly? I am glad you asked:

Music channels: Play/Pause and skip

Podcast episodes: 15 sec back, Play/Pause and 30 sec forward

Radio stations: Play/Pause

These were selected as the most often used player controls that I can display along the current stream thumbnail and labels.

Since I am displayed as part of the footer overlay, as mentioned before, I use an inverse color scheme to display my elements in the most legible way. This means that I look like a dark mode UI element when the app is light mode and vice versa.

In a nutshell, I am very proud to be the optimized representative of the full screen player on almost every other screen of the app so I can always be there for users of all types to quickly and easily interact with their currently streaming content. And at the same time, I am also a most helpful and accessible shortcut to my older brother’s full screen display.
