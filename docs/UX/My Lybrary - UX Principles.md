# SM My Library – UX Principles

## Purpose

My Library is the central hub for all user‑driven content and account-related information. It consolidates personalized media, activity history, and app settings into a single, easy‑to‑access destination.

---

## Core UX Principles

### 1. Unified Hub for User Ownership

- My Library brings together all user‑specific content across music, podcasts, and radio, along with account and app information.
- It acts as the single source of truth for anything tied to user activity or identity.

**UX intent:** Reinforce a strong sense of ownership by centralizing everything that belongs to the user.

---

### 2. Clear Separation Between Content and System Information

- App-related items (Account, FAQ, Contact, About) are grouped in a dedicated swimlane.
- This swimlane is visually distinguished with a gear icon and positioned at the top.

**UX intent:** Help users quickly differentiate between personal content and system-level actions.

---

### 3. Progressive Disclosure Through Usage

- The Library grows as users interact with the app.
- Initially, only key history swimlanes are visible; additional categories appear as they become relevant.

**UX intent:** Prevent overwhelming users while creating a sense of progression and personalization.

---

### 4. Chronological Continuity

- History swimlanes (Music, Podcasts, Radio) display content in reverse chronological order.
- Ensures immediate access to recently consumed content.

**UX intent:** Prioritize recency to support quick resumption and habitual usage patterns.

---

### 5. Familiar Content Grouping Patterns

- Content is consistently organized by type and intent:
  - History → Favorites / Subscriptions → In Progress → Saved → Downloads
- The same structure is repeated across music, podcasts, and radio.

**UX intent:** Build predictable mental models across content types to reduce learning effort.

---

### 6. Actionable Collection Surfaces

- Key swimlanes provide direct pathways to deeper control:
  - Example: Music History always exposes a “More” entry point for full management (including clearing history).
- Collections are not just for browsing—they support management actions.

**UX intent:** Empower users to actively manage their content, not just view it.

---

### 7. Contextual Density and Relevance

- Not all swimlanes are always visible; they appear only when populated.
- Avoids empty states except where necessary (e.g., initial history placeholders).

**UX intent:** Keep the interface relevant and reduce noise by showing only meaningful content.

---

### 8. Gradual Personalization Over Time

- My Library evolves from a minimal state into a rich, personalized environment.
- The experience becomes more valuable as user engagement increases.

**UX intent:** Encourage continued usage by visibly rewarding engagement with richer content.

---

### 9. Balanced Information Architecture

- Combines a large volume of personalized content under a single screen using structured swimlanes.
- Maintains navigability despite content breadth through grouping and hierarchy.

**UX intent:** Scale complexity without sacrificing clarity or usability.

---

### 10. Immediate Access to Account and Support

- Account status and support resources are always accessible from the top of the screen.
- Includes Account & Settings, FAQ, Contact Us, and About.

**UX intent:** Ensure critical user actions and information are always easy to find, even if infrequently used.

---

## In Summary

My Library is designed as the user’s personal command center: a progressively enriching space where content, activity, and account context converge. It balances depth and clarity through structured grouping, adaptive visibility, and strong organizational consistency, ensuring users always feel in control of their experience.
