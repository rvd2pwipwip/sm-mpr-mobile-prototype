# SM Home Screen – UX Principles

## Purpose

These UX principles define the experience foundations for the Stingray Music Home screen. They guide design, product, and engineering decisions to ensure the Home screen consistently acts as the app’s **primary ambassador**, **daily-use hub**, and **main personalization surface**.

---

## 1. Be Immediately Welcoming

**The Home screen must clearly communicate value within seconds, especially for guest users.**

- Present curated Music, Podcast, and Radio content without requiring prior knowledge.
- Use the header, branding, and content order to explain *what the app is* and *what users can do*.
- Avoid cognitive overload by establishing clear visual hierarchy from top to bottom.

**Success signal**: New users understand the app’s offer and start listening without searching.

---

## 2. Reflect the User at All Times

**The Home screen must visibly adapt to who the user is.**

- Header, footer, ads, and available actions must always align with the current user type:
  - Guest  
  - freeStingray  
  - freeProvider  
  - Subscribed
- Users should instantly recognize their status (upgrade available, provider access, or full access).

**Success signal**: Users never question why they see certain CTAs, logos, or ads.

---

## 3. Prioritize Continuity of Listening

**Returning to previously played content is more important than discovering new content.**

- The **Listen Again** swimlane must surface as soon as listening history exists.
- Mixed content types (channels, podcasts, stations) are acceptable and expected.
- Smaller tiles and no labels reduce friction and emphasize recognition over exploration.

**Success signal**: Users routinely resume listening from the Home screen.

---

## 4. Personalization Earns Its Prominence

**Personalized content should appear only when it is meaningful.**

- Favorites appear high but only when populated.
- Recommendations start generic and evolve with usage.
- Personalization should feel *earned*, not forced or empty.

**Success signal**: Personalized swimlanes feel relevant rather than repetitive or blank.

---

## 5. Promote Without Disrupting

**Marketing content must coexist gracefully with editorial content.**

- The self‑promo banner is highly visible but does not block primary actions.
- Ads are integrated between swimlanes or in the footer, never interrupting playback or navigation.
- Paid users enjoy a clean, uninterrupted experience.

**Success signal**: Promotion feels intentional, not intrusive.

---

## 6. Maintain a Predictable Structure

**Consistency in layout builds speed, confidence, and comfort.**

- Swimlanes always follow the same visual and behavioral patterns.
- Titles and “More” actions set clear expectations.
- Vertical scrolling is uninterrupted by fixed elements.

**Success signal**: Users instinctively know where to look and how to act.

---

## 7. Respect Content Visibility

**No content should ever be obscured by UI chrome.**

- Footer height dynamically adjusts based on ads and the miniplayer.
- Content containers always account for current footer state.
- Layout recalculations are invisible to the user.

**Success signal**: All content remains fully visible and reachable.

---

## 8. Reduce the Need to Search

**The Home screen should satisfy most daily needs without extra effort.**

- Common listening paths start and end on Home.
- Discovery, resumption, and promotion coexist without conflict.
- Search remains available but optional.

**Success signal**: Regular users rely on Home as their primary navigation surface.

---

## 9. Design for Linear and On‑Demand Equally

**Music, radio, and podcasts must feel equally first‑class.**

- Linear content emphasizes quick restart and familiarity.
- Podcast access prioritizes return to shows rather than episode micromanagement.
- Content type differences never break the layout logic.

**Success signal**: All content types feel native and intentional.

---

## 10. Earn Trust Through Clarity

**Transparency builds comfort, especially with monetization.**

- Upgrade CTAs are clear and honest.
- Ads are visually distinguishable from editorial content.
- The absence of ads for subscribed users reinforces value.

**Success signal**: Users trust the Home screen and its intentions.

---

## Guiding Question

When in doubt, ask:

> *“Does this make the Home screen more welcoming, more personal, and easier to use than before?”*

If the answer is no, the decision likely violates one or more of these principles.
